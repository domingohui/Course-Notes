1 First-order Differential Equations

Definition: A differential equation (DE), is an equation involving unknown functions and their derivative(s).
e.g. $\frac{dy}{dx}=y(x)$
Solutions: $y(x)=e^x, y(x)=0$
General Solution: $y(x)=Ce^x$

Falling Object with Air Resistance

$$m\frac{dv}{dt}=mg-F_d$$ At low speeds, $F_d\propto v$: $F_d=\alpha v\quad\alpha > 0$ is a constant.
At high speeds, $F_d\propto v^2$: $F_d=\beta v|v|\quad\beta > 0$ is a constant.

We will focus on the linear drag law, so $\frac{dv}{dt}=g-\frac{\alpha}{m}v$.
Note that all velocities converge to $\frac{mg}{\alpha}$, which is known as the terminal velocity.

Dimensions of Quantities

Dimensional Analysis is a useful method for finding relationships between variables in an equations and ensuring the equation makes sense. Main Dimensions:
  • M - Mass
  • L - Length
  • T - Time
  • $\theta$ - Temperature
e.g. $[v]=$"dimension of velocity"$=\frac{L}{T}=LT^{-1}$, $\ [F]=MLT^{-2}$, $\ [\frac{v}{c}]=1=$"dimensionless" Principle of Dimensional Homogeneity: Addition/subtraction/equation of terms must have the same units/dimensions.
e.g. Find the dimensions of $\alpha$. $$[mg]=[\alpha v]\rightarrow MLT^{-2}=[\alpha]LT^{-1}\rightarrow [\alpha]=MT^{-1}$$
